Jay N. Chaudhari

Introduction

Jay is a junior research fellow at Ahmedabad University working on GUJCOST Project, Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance under the guidance of Prof. Mehul S Raval, Ahmedabad University and Prof. Paawan Sharma, Pandit Deendayal University, Gandhinagar. He received his Master of Engineering in Automatic Control and Robotics from The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda, India.

His research interests include computer vision, model compression, and edge AI. His primary focus is on developing vision-language models and deploying them on low resource constrained devices.

Experience

  • Junior Research Fellow, Ahmedabad University (2022 - Present)
    • Project: Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance
    • Project funded by Gujarat Council on Science and Technology, Govt. of Gujarat
    • The project aims to develop a person retrieval system that processes a natural language query, processes the query through video frames, and retrieves the person in the surveillance footage.
    • The major focus of the project is its deployment on low-resource constrained devices.

Projects

  • Title: Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance

    Led the project focused on creating an end-to-end system for person retrieval in video surveillance.

    Developed a novel person attribute recognition model which developed correlation between the attributes.

    Scaled down the model 10 times using model compression techniques.

    Successfully deployed model on Nvidia Jetson Orion hardware for real-time video surveillance applications.

  • Title: Sleep Stage Classification Using Machine Learning Algorithms

    • Developed a machine learning model for sleep stage classification, using Gradient Boosting, Decision Trees, and Bootstrap Aggregating algorithms.
    • Employed Wavelet decomposition of EEG Signal to extract features and incorporated time domain features.
    • Addressed the issue of imbalanced labels in the dataset, experimenting with both under-sampling and over-sampling techniques.
    • Achieved a high accuracy rate of 91% through the application of wavelet decomposition for feature extraction.

  • Title: Energy Management of Hybrid Wind Battery System

    • Aimed to reduce financial penalties incurred by the grid operator due to inaccuracies in wind speed forecasts.
    • Implemented support vector regression, random forest, ARIMA, and simple exponential smoothing forecasting models.
    • Developed a battery charging and discharging scheduling algorithm based on state of charge and battery life.
    • Optimized power utilization from the battery storage system, resulting in a significant improvement of up to 40%.

Introduction

Jay is a junior research fellow at Ahmedabad University working on the GUJCOST Project, Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance under the guidance of Prof. Mehul S Raval at Ahmedabad University and Prof. Paawan Sharma at Pandit Deendayal Energy University, Gandhinagar. He received his Master of Engineering in Automatic Control and Robotics from The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda, India.

His research interests include computer vision, model compression, and edge AI. His primary focus is on developing vision-language models and deploying them on low-resource constrained devices.

Experience

    • Project funded by Gujarat Council on Science and Technology, Govt. of Gujarat
    • The project aims to develop a person retrieval system that processes a natural language query, processes the query through video frames, and retrieves the person in the surveillance footage.

Projects

  • Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance

    • Led the project focused on creating an end-to-end system for person retrieval in video surveillance.
    • Developed a novel person attribute recognition model, establishing correlations between attributes.
    • Implemented model compression techniques, reducing its size by 10 times.
    • Successfully deployed the model on Nvidia Jetson Orion hardware for real-time video surveillance applications.

  • Sleep Stage Classification Using Machine Learning Algorithms

    • Designed and developed a machine learning model for sleep stage classification, employing Gradient Boosting, Decision Trees, and Bootstrap Aggregating algorithms.
    • Utilized Wavelet decomposition of EEG Signal to extract features, integrating time domain features for improved accuracy.
    • Tackled imbalanced labels in the dataset through extensive experimentation with under-sampling and over-sampling techniques.
    • Achieved an outstanding accuracy rate of 91% through the application of wavelet decomposition for feature extraction.

  • Energy Management of Hybrid Wind Battery System

    • Directed efforts to reduce financial penalties caused by inaccuracies in wind speed forecasts, focusing on hybrid wind-battery systems.
    • Implemented a suite of forecasting models including support vector regression, random forest, ARIMA, and simple exponential smoothing.
    • Formulated a robust battery charging and discharging scheduling algorithm based on state of charge and battery life considerations.
    • Optimized power utilization from the battery storage system, resulting in a remarkable improvement of up to 40% in energy efficiency.
